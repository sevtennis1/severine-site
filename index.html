I'm a behavioral economist interested in understanding how people make decisions--especially when those decisions involve temptation, self-control, and uncertainty. My research spans behavioral decision theory, public health applications (particularly commitment devices for healthy choices), and metascience. I'm passionate about research transparency and enjoy exploring the "behind the scenes" of academic work.
